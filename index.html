Places where AEW has been



All Elite Wrestling was founded in 2019. It's inaugural event was their Double or Nothing Pay-Per-View event, which took place at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas, Nevada on May 25, 2019.

Here's all the places AEW have been to since then. The most visited states were Florida, Illinois, and Texas. Most of the AEW Dynamite events have taken place in the Eastern Central and East Coast.

AEW will be returning to the West Coast for their Double or Nothing Pay Per View event on May 23rd, 2020 which will be held once again at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Paradise, Nevada.





Wrestling Rankings



  • When looking at most wins in singles matches, Jon Moxley takes the lead the highest percentage of wins as well as the greatest number of singles matches. He's never loss a singles match and only faced a time limit draw once during a match against PAC on Dynamite on Oct 23, 2019. In fact, he's only been on the losing side once, and that was during a tag team match the week before on Dynamite on Oct 16, 2019, where Jon Moxley and PAC loss against Kenny Omega and Adam Page.
  • When looking at most wins considering all matches, Kenny Omega takes the lead with 17 wins out of 24 matches.
  • In the female division, Riho and Nyla Rose have the greatest number of wins in singles matches and have only incurred one loss and that was to each other.

  • Joey Janela has the second greatest number of singles matches and also the greatest number of losses, with 7 losses out of 11 matches.
  • Brandon Cutler (0/6) and Leva Bates (0/4) leads with most losses and zero wins in singles matches for male and female divsion, respectively.
  • When looking at total matches, Peter Avalon and Brandon Cutler are both 0 for 9.





Most Utilized Wrestlers



  • Kenny Omega has been in the most number of matches in the ring, followed by Scorpio Sky, Trent, Nick Jason, and Adam Page.
  • Even though Chris Jericho appears frequently in all things AEW, he's only been in the ring 12 times.

  • As mentioned earlier, Jon Moxley and Joey Janela have been in the most singles matches to date.
  • Sammy Guevara, PAC, Kenny Omega, and Cody have all wrestler the same number of singles matches. Sammy Guevara has the greatest percentage of losses.
  • PAC and Kenny Omega's statistics for singles matches are similar, but Kenny Omega is also very involved in tag team matches with his partner Adam Page.

  • Wrestlers with most non-singles matches are Scorpio Sky, Nick and Matt Jackson, Frankie Kazarian, Chuck Taylor, and Trent, representing the tag teams So Cal Uncensored, The Young Bucks, and The Best Friends, respectively.






Matches Times

  • Longest singles match recorded was at PPV Full Gear on November 9, 2019 between Jon Moxley and Kenny Omega. Match lasted 38 mins 46 secs.
  • Longest singles match aired on Dynamite was between Cody and Darby Allin on January 1, 2020 Episode 13. Match lasted 17 mins 18 secs.

  • Shortest singles match occured on December 11, 2019 during Dynamite episode 11. Jon Moxley defeated Alex Reynolds in 14 secs.

  • Most of the longest matches were Pay Per View headlining or main event matches.



Wrestler Total Match Times

  • Kenny Omega has the greatest total time in singles matches with 3 hours 13 minutes and 54 seconds. The average Kenny Omega singles match lasts about 21.5 minutes.
  • Although Chris Jericho has not been in many singles matches, he has the second greatest average time per match, suggesting that when he does go in the ring alone, don't expect the match to end quickly. His average match time is just under 20 minutes.
  • The average Cody singles match is just under 16 minutes.
  • The average Jon Moxley singles match is about 12.5 minutes.

  • Wrestlers with most in ring time during Dynamite are Adam Page, Kenny Omega, Nick Jackson, Scorpio Sky, and Matt Jackson.
  • Wrestlers with most in ring time during Dark are Jack Evans, Trent, Chuck Taylor, Kenny Omega, and Marko Stunt.
  • Kenny Omega is everywhere.






Wednesday Night Wars



AEW's Dynamite debuted on the TNT network on October 2, 2019. Since then, AEW and WWE's NXT have been battling for viewership Wednesday nights with Dynamite airing on TNT and NXT airing on USA Network. While it is debatable to whether Nielson ratings are still a relevant performance indicator, there is still interest in comparing the viewership and Nielson ratings between WWE NXT and AEW Dynamite.

WarnerMedia announced on January 15th, 2020 that TNT's AEW Dynamite has been renewed through 2023. In addition, they will also be launching a second series on another night, details to come.

Recently, Cody Rhodes spoke in an interview about the future of AEW and the potential of moving the show to a different night to compete against a difference WWE brand. Link to the interview can be found here.

About



Data was retrieved from Cagematch The Internet Wrestling Database (https://www.cagematch.net/). The scraping was performed using Python, Beautiful Soup, and Splinter. The data was then written to JSON files and imported into Tableau for analysis.

All matches aired on Pay Per View events, Dynamite, and Dark are counted in the data. This includes unsanctioned matches, battle royals, and unofficial matches. Note that this differs from how AEW officially computes their rankings.
